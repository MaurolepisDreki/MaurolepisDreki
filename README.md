## Liberty
To me there is nothing more valuable than Liberty.  Cases have been and continue to be made on how much Liberty a person is 
entitled to have, and my stance on the subject is that if there is any Liberty to be had which does not infringe on the Liberty
or Property of others then it is theirs by Right of Birth under Natural Law.  Many have come to justify such infringements with
claims that their enemies' actions do instead infringe upon themselves be it the consumption of recreational pharmasudicals or
being unpelasant on the senses or most recently being of controversial opinion or otherwise unconformant to their own limited
view of what the world should be; such persons are the true enemies of Liberty.

There really isn't much I can say that has not already been said, for I surely stand on the shoulders of giants, but there can
be no doubt that the key principals of Libtery are Tolerance, Understanding, and Respect in their every conceivable meaning.

### Contract Theory
This theory of community and it's origins are derived from Liberty and is essentialy that any authority derived by any entity
over another is contingent on the one submitting to the other to acheive some common purpose.  Most commonly this is applied to
Statedomes where the contract essentialy is that the government provides protection from rouge factions' and other States'
interfearance with the Liberty and Property of it's Citizens in exchange for fealty, goods, and services by which it supports
itself; and when that contract breaks down such that the people loose faith in their governments' ability to protect them,
especialy from itself, that government looses it's authority and ultimately ceases to exist.  Nevertheless, Contract Theory is
applicable in every relationship including those between employer and employee and those between significant others.

#### My Business Contract
**As an employee, I dedicate fourty hours out of every week to my employer at regular times of their choosing to perform 
specific, predetermined, tasks according to my skill and abillity, &mdash; including by which means I see fit as becomes
nessisary when troubles arrise, &mdash; and in return I expect to receive compensation for my labors in the form of legal
tinder at an agreed upon rate, to not be required to perform any task outside of said hours for any reason on behalf of my
employer, and to not suffer any residual effects of those tasks required by my employer.**

Pretty standard, in my oppinion: I do work, I get paid, I go live my own life.  Turns out, however, that most people don't
agree on some point of this philosophy whether it is on them doing work, matters of payment, or having a life that doesn't
involve the company, and so I'm starting to realize that I have to spell it out for people because they tend to assume anything
but this contract:

__Anything more than 40 hours in a week is unacceptable:__ to think yourself entitled to more of my time is like having your
car stolen and the theif throwing money at you to make you even.  You needed that car to use, but the theif doesn't care and

gave you to do so, especialy to buy the exact replacement of that vehicle in all it's mechanical glory.  It is not a fair
trade, and certainly not one mutualy agreed upon by both parties.  And if you kill my dog while your stealing my car, you're

__Getting paied for anything less than 40 hours a week is also unacceptable:__ I promised you 40 hours of my time, time that I
could have sold to someone else and will probably spend looking for another buyer, just like you would when you hold a product

__Interfering with my life outside of work is completely intolerable:__ doesn't really matter what it is: my health is none of

## Quality \[over Quantity\]
*"Do it right, or do it again."*

One thing I never quite figured out is which idiot coined the phrase "Quality, or else."  Here in the real world Murphy's Law
is practically de facto: *Anything that can go wrong will go wrong.*  And that being the case, you only have two choices: spend
time fixing the problems as they arrise, or ignore them untill they come back with a vengance later.  That's it!  You can't do
both!

I'm sure that revelation just caused a few managers to die of shock while the rest are safe within the realm of denial.  But
even so, at the end of the day you have to choose whether to build a quality product that makes people want to use it or a
quantity product that people only use because it is so readily available.  In my experiance managers do lip-service to the
former while enforcing the latter, and I don't even want to know how it came to this.

To me, quality means making your customer experiance so transparent that they barely realize they are using your product at
all.  Truly, if your interface is simple and intuitive, and your code ran without ever throwing errors or slowing the user down
in any way, would you even realize you where using it?  Probably not, because that application would quickly become an
extenstion of yourself such that you begin to use it without thinking as a solution to your problems.  Windows users probably
don't know what I'm talking about as Microsoft's policy has been "Just change the UI and rename everything, our users won't
know the difference between that and a real update" for as long as I can remember, but I assure you that it is quite possible
to know your software so well that you start giving the computer commands faster than it can accept them (I do it all the time).

As Programmer my emphasis on Quality is mainly expressed in terms of UX, Bugs, and Digital Footprinting; I know full well that
there is more aspects that I could target, but I believe that if I do well on these fronts, and do them well, then I will have
no issues in aquiring and maintaing customers (employers asside) because I will have a superior product that is stable enough
be droped into production environments without long-term repurcussions, the very thing avery consumer needs but has forgotten
that they need.
